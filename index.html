14 Juillet — Bastille Day

Fête Nationale in Annecy

July 14th is France's biggest national holiday. Annecy celebrates with the full programme — expect crowds, festivity, and a spectacular setting over the lake.

Fireworks over the Lake
Map
FreeDon't Miss

The fireworks display over Lac d'Annecy is one of the most beautiful in France. Best viewing spots: Jardins de l'Europe, Pâquier, and the hillside above town. Arrive early (by 9pm) to get a lakeside spot — it fills up completely. Show typically starts around 10:30pm.

✓ Saved
Military Parade — Old Town
Map
Free

A traditional morning parade through the old town with local bands, municipal guards, and civic associations. Starts around 10am. The narrow streets of the Vieille Ville make for a very atmospheric setting.

✓ Saved
Bal Populaire (Street Dancing)
Map
Free

Open-air dancing in the town squares and around the lake — a tradition on Bastille night. Live music, outdoor bars, and the whole town in celebration mode from the evening until after midnight.

✓ Saved
Boat on the Lake for Fireworks
Map
Book far aheadSpecial

Watching the 14 Juillet fireworks from a boat on the lake is extraordinary — reflected colours on the water with the mountains as backdrop. FAM BOAT and local operators run special evening cruises. These sell out weeks in advance — book immediately.

✓ Saved
Lake Cruise Special — Compagnie des Bateaux
Map
Paid ~€25–40

The official lake company runs a special Bastille Day evening cruise with on-board dinner option. Tickets include a prime position for the fireworks. Check their website for the 2025 programme — usually released a month in advance.

✓ Saved
⚠️ 14 Juillet tips: Most shops and some restaurants close on the 14th. Stock up on food the day before. Roads around the lake get extremely congested — arrive on foot or by bike. The morning of the 14th is a great time to hike (quieter trails, festive atmosphere in town when you return).

Hiking (no car needed)

Bus or boat shuttle connects all trailheads. Start early — trails get hot by midday in summer.
Mont Veyrier / Mont Baron Loop
Map
Medium–HardFree4–5 hrs

Walk or bus to the trailhead (1hr on foot from centre). Loop via Col des Sauts, Mont Veyrier, Mont Baron, and old cable car ruins. Panoramic lake views. Rocky sections — proper shoes and poles recommended.

✓ Saved
Sentier de la Crête
Map
Easy–ModerateFree2.5–3 hrs

Starts near Annecy train station. Loop through Massif des Bauges Regional Natural Park. Mostly shaded — ideal in summer heat. Several viewpoints over the lake.

✓ Saved
Angon Waterfall (from Talloires)
Map
EasyFree1–1.5 hrs

Bus or boat to Talloires. Short, shaded trail carved into rock leading to a spectacular waterfall. Can be slippery near the end — watch your step. Great for a half-day trip combined with a swim at Talloires beach.

✓ Saved
Laudon Circuit (from Saint-Jorioz)
Map
ModerateFree3.5–4 hrs

Bus to Saint-Jorioz. Beautiful alpine pasture, forest, and mountain villages. One of the most scenic bus-accessible hikes around the lake.

✓ Saved
⏰ Tip: In peak summer, start at sunrise and aim to be off exposed trails by early afternoon. The shuttle boat connects villages so you're not limited to trails starting in Annecy.

Map
No Licence

Up to 8 people. Central table — perfect for a picnic or apéritif on the water. Silent, zero emissions, swim ladder. 1.5, 2, or 3-hour slots. Book in advance in summer.

✓ Saved
FAM BOAT — Les Marquisats
Map
No Licence

Up to 6 people, departing from central Annecy. Fuel + briefing included. Bring a picnic, swim from the boat, explore freely. April–September. Free cancellation in bad weather.

Map
No Licence

Available at Annecy-le-Vieux, Sévrier, Saint-Jorioz, Duingt, Talloires, and more. Some models have a slide. ID required as deposit. Open spring – end September.

Savoyard Specialties to Buy

These are found in any supermarché (Carrefour, Casino, Leclerc) or better at a local épicerie or the market. Great to bring home as gifts — or eat on the spot.
Grocery Picks
🧀
Reblochon de Savoie AOP
The king of local cheeses. Creamy, nutty, mildly pungent. Buy a whole round vacuum-packed for travel. Essential for tartiflette.
🧀
Beaufort AOP
A firm mountain cheese with a fruity depth — sometimes called the "Prince of Gruyères." Excellent for slicing and snacking.
🧀
Tomme de Savoie
Mild semi-firm cheese with a distinctive grey rind. Widely available and very affordable. Great melted or eaten plain.
🍷
Vin de Savoie (Apremont, Chignin)
Light Alpine white wines from grapes grown near the lake. Apremont pairs perfectly with cheese and lake fish. Very reasonably priced locally.
🫙
Génépi
Herbal Alpine liqueur made from artemisia plants. Traditional digestif of Savoie. Small bottles travel well. Also available as syrup for non-drinkers.
🍯
Miel de Savoie (Mountain Honey)
Floral, complex, and wildly different from supermarket honey. Acacia and wildflower varieties are the most common. Sold in small jars at the market.
🥩
Diots de Savoie
Traditional Savoyard pork sausages, smoked or fresh. Often served with polenta or in white wine. Buy at the market or any boucherie.
🌰
Crozets de Savoie
Tiny buckwheat or wheat pasta squares. A Savoyard staple — cook gratin-style with Beaufort and cream. Lightweight to pack and take home.
🍫
Chocolat des Alpes
Several Savoie-based chocolatiers produce alpine herb chocolates — look for génépi ganache, salted butter caramel, and mountain berry varieties.
🧂
Sel de l'Alpe / Herbes de Savoie
Local spice and herb blends for seasoning cheeses and meats. Small bags make ideal and very compact souvenirs.
✓ Saved
📦 Taking cheese home? Vacuum-pack it at the shop (most fromageries offer this). Keep in your checked bag if flying. Reblochon and Beaufort travel well for 2–3 days unrefrigerated if vacuum-packed.

Planning Tips

Getting Around

Buses run all around the lake for ~€2. The Vélonecy e-bike scheme covers most city routes — free first 30 min, re-dock to reset. Boat shuttles connect villages in summer (check Compagnie des Bateaux schedule). A car is only needed for more remote trails.

✓ Saved
Beat the Crowds

Old town and Palais de l'Île: visit before 9am or after 7pm. Beaches: arrive before 10am on weekends. Market: get there early for the best produce before the crowds (and the sun) peak.

✓ Saved
Weather & Mountains

Mountain weather can change quickly. On hikes, check the forecast the night before — afternoon thunderstorms are common in summer. The lake surface is calm most mornings, choppier in the afternoon wind.

✓ Saved
📲 Useful apps: Vélonecy (e-bikes) · Compagnie des Bateaux (lake shuttles) · SIBRA (local buses) · IGNrando or Komoot (trails) · Google Maps offline download covers the whole area well.
